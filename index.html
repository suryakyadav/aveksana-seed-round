The Logic of Inquiry

We don't just search papers. We enforce a sequence of non-negotiable logical decisions.

Pillar 1

Define

Premise Selection

Move from a general topic to a specific problem. What exact logical gap justifies this research?

Output:

A clearly articulated problem statement grounded in literature.

Pillar 2

Narrow

Bounding & Scope

Make the research achievable. What constraints define where this study starts and ends?

Output:

A bounded scope aligned with time, data, and resources.

Pillar 3

Identify

The Contribution

Crystallize why this work matters. What is the specific contribution relative to existing work?

Output:

A validated contribution statement that survives scrutiny.

Pillar 4

Validate

Structural Integrity

Confirm the logic forms a defensible whole. Is it stable enough to support drafting?

Output:

A mentor-ready logic framework (Blueprint for the manuscript).
